Kabaddi

Kabaddi is a contact team sport that originated in India and is especially popular in South Asia. Played between two teams of seven, it involves players taking turns to raid the opponent's side and tag as many players as possible without being caught.

The Pro Kabaddi League has significantly boosted the sport's popularity in India. The game demands strength, strategy, and quick reflexes.

Kabaddi is also a part of the Asian Games and has growing viewership globally.
